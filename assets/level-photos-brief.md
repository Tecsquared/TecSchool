# Journey-Slider Level Photos — Art-Direction Brief

**Where these go:** the P5 journey slider (the page's *peak* moment) — one photo per level, in the card's photo slot (currently the `[Real classroom photo: …]` placeholder).
**Strategic job:** make the 10-year pathway feel real and prospective — *"this could be my child."* Realism is the point. Do **not** stylise these as illustrations; do **not** use stock / AI "happy diverse kids." Real TEC kids only (per the brief).

---

## The core rule

Each photo shows the **capability that level builds** — not a generic posed child. The image should visually prove that level's descriptor.

Curate as **one consistent set**, not 15 random snapshots. Consistency beats individual shot quality here — 15 mismatched photos look worse than anything.

**Prefer activity-focused, face-light framing** (hands forming letters, a finger tracking a line of a book, over-the-shoulder at the writing, a teen mid-gesture from behind). This:
- Lightens the PDPA/consent burden (less identifiable)
- Depicts the *capability* more directly than a posed smile
- Auto-unifies the set (every shot is "child + the work," not 15 different faces)

## Treatment

- **One consistent grade** across all 15 — same light feel, same crop logic, same warmth.
- **Per-pillar colour wash (recommended):** a subtle duotone/tint matching the stage's pillar — Play to Learn green, Reader to Leader blue, Build Your Future orange. The slider already shifts `--stage-color` per pillar; the photo tone moves with it as the parent scrubs, reinforcing the three-act structure that's now also in the tag rows.
- **Alternative:** all on the warm paper ground (`#FCFBF6`, like the founder portraits + parrot thumbnail). Maximum cohesion, less dynamic.
- Pick one treatment and apply to all 15 — never mix.

## Technical (set precisely at wiring time)

- Landscape, consistent aspect ratio (matched to the slider card's photo box).
- Web-optimised like the founder portraits (~500–700px, ~30–70KB each, lazy-loaded).
- Same filename convention, e.g. `assets/level-photos/01-teeny-tec.jpg` … `15-advanced-2.jpg`.

## PDPA / consent

Real identifiable children require documented parental consent (under-10s especially). Activity-focused / face-light framing reduces this burden. Consent is the school's responsibility before any photo ships publicly.
